What this illustrates

  • Broad labels: Collapsing distinct identities into “Minority” erases distinctions and can mislead AI/ML models.
  • Hidden differences: Aggregate rates for “Minority” can hide outcomes for specific groups (e.g., Native American veterans).
  • Sampling bias: Small or under-sampled groups make averages unstable, amplifying error, and stereotype risk.
  • Ethical takeaway: Use specific, culturally respectful labels and evaluate outcomes per group before collecting data.
